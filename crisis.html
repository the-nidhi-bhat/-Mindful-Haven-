Crisis Safety Plan

If you're experiencing a mental health crisis, follow these steps to keep yourself safe.

1

Remove immediate danger

Move away from any means of self-harm. Put physical distance between you and anything that could be dangerous. Go to a safe, comfortable space.

2

Call a crisis helpline immediately

Dial 1800-599-0019 (KIRAN), 9999-666-555 (Vandrevala), or 112 (Emergency). Trained professionals are available 24/7 to help you through this moment.

3

Contact someone you trust

Call or message a family member, friend, teacher, or counselor. Don't face this alone‚Äîlet someone know how you're feeling right now.

4

Go to a safe environment

Move to a public place, be with others, or go to the nearest hospital emergency department. Being around people can help you feel safer.

5

Use grounding techniques

Try the 5-4-3-2-1 method: Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. This helps bring you back to the present moment.

When to Seek Immediate Help

Reach out for professional support if you or someone you know is experiencing any of these warning signs:

Thoughts of suicide or self-harm
Making plans to end your life or researching methods
Feeling trapped, hopeless, or like there's no way out
Severe anxiety, panic attacks, or overwhelming emotional pain
Sudden withdrawal from friends, family, or activities you once enjoyed
Dramatic mood swings or personality changes
Giving away belongings or saying goodbye in unusual ways
Increased substance use or reckless behavior

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to questions you might have about reaching out for help.

Is calling a helpline really confidential?
Yes, all crisis helplines maintain strict confidentiality. Your call is private, and counselors will not share your information unless you're in immediate danger and need emergency services. You can also choose to remain anonymous.
What if I'm not sure if my situation is "serious enough"?
If you're struggling, that's serious enough. Crisis helplines are here for anyone experiencing distress, not just those in immediate danger. Counselors are trained to help with a wide range of concerns, from acute crisis to emotional support.
What happens when I call a crisis helpline?
A trained counselor will answer and listen to what you're going through without judgment. They'll help you work through your feelings, develop a safety plan, and connect you with additional resources if needed. The conversation is led by you‚Äîshare what you're comfortable with.
Can I text or WhatsApp instead of calling?
Yes! Vandrevala Foundation (9999-666-555) accepts calls, SMS, and WhatsApp. KIRAN (1800-599-0019) also has text options. If you're more comfortable texting, that's a completely valid way to reach out.
